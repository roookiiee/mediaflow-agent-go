# Video Localization Playbook

Short-form localization should preserve intent rather than literal sentence order. For TikTok and Shorts, the first three seconds need a clear hook, the next ten seconds should prove the payoff, and the ending should contain one concrete action.

TTS scripts need shorter clauses than written copy. A practical target is one idea per sentence, fewer than 16 English words per sentence, and explicit pronunciation notes for product names, acronyms, and numbers.

Voice cloning is a consent-gated capability. The workflow should require proof that the speaker owns or has licensed the target voice, keep a reviewer approval step, and provide a neutral preset voice fallback.

Subtitles should be generated with stable segment IDs so the team can edit text without invalidating audio timing. The product should track subtitle drift, failed render count, and reviewer edits per minute.
